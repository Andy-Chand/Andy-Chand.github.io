About Me:

After completing my B.Sc. at Wilfrid Laurier University, I acquired a Biotechnology Post-Graduate Diploma from Durham College, where I also worked in an aquatic toxicology lab preparing media. Toxicology lab work led me to the Quality Assurance and Quality Control (QA/QC) field. I switched to Geographic Information Systems (GIS) because it incorporated my interests in data visualization, computer programming, data management, and design, while keeping up quality standards.



Taking the Geographic Information Systems post-graduate certificate through Fanshawe College, I have also picked up GIS-specific skills, including:

  • Updating and handling GIS data to produce maps and figures,
  • Learning various programming languages (Python, SQL, R, C#, HTML, and Javascript),
  • Experience using CAD software (AutoCAD Civil3D),
  • Managing, updating, and modifying databases, and
  • Developing programs to handle and showcase data from various source.



Through my previous years of work experience in the QA/QC field and as a supervisor, I developed numerous transferrable skills to apply to a GIS Specialist role. These transferable skills include:

  • Project management skills,
  • Excellent oral and written communication skills,
  • Strong organizational and time management skills,
  • Attention to detail,
  • Auditing data and reports,
  • The ability to work independently and within a team environment,
  • Leading teams, and
  • Self-motivated and problem-solving abilities.
